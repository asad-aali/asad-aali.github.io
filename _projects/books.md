## Historical Biographies

<table>
	<tr>
		<td> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/promised_land.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td> 
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/55361205-a-promised-land">A Promised Land</a> <br> by Barack Obama </td>

	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td> <br> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/team_of_rivals.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>

		<td> Gripping account of Lincoln's character and empathy leading a contentious group of his talented political rivals through the Civil War. I found his extraordinary emotional intelligence and leadership skills very inspiring. </td>
	</tr>
</table>

## Memoirs

<table>
	<tr>
		<td> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/mans_search_for_meaning.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td> 
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/4069.Man_s_Search_for_Meaning?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=NpgSbAvOlQ&rank=1">Man's Search for Meaning</a> <br> by Victor Frankl </td>
		<td> Riveting memoir of Nazi death camps and the ensuing lessons learned during Frankl's career as a psychiatrist. It was a striking demonstration of the human ability to choose how we experience life and define purpose, even amidst grueling circumstances. </td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td> <br> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/when_breath.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>

		<td> Reflections on the meaning of life from a neurosurgeon&mdash;who himself has advised hundreds of patients facing death&mdash;as he grapples with his own terminal diagnosis. I admired Kalanithi for seeking to focus on what truly matters amidst the distractions of a competitive career landscape. </td>
	</tr>
</table>

## Poetry

<table>
	<tr>
		<td> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/the_prophet.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/2547.The_Prophet?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=0FrqSrXGAD&rank=1">The Prophet</a> <br> by Kahlil Gibran </td>

		<td> Lyrical translations of the 13th-century Sufi Mystic. Rumi's abstract metaphors originate from an entirely different time and culture. Sometimes I sift through to find profound insight; other times I humbly miss the point&mdash;or come to accept that perhaps there isn't one. </td>
	</tr>

## Social Psychology 

<table>
	<tr>
		<td> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/righteous_mind.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11324722-the-righteous-mind?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=aSfXJxl1XX&rank=1">The Righteous Mind</a> <br> by Jonathan Haidt </td>
		<td> Research examining moral intuition and its impact on the sociopolitical landscape&mdash;particularly relevant given today's individualized information streams. Personally, reading this enabled deeper understanding and empathy for people whose views seem so mind-bogglingly different from my own. </td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td> <br> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/think_again.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td> 
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/55539565-think-again?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=NkKAI267FG&rank=2">Think Again</a> <br> by Adam Grant </td>
		<td> The critical art of re-thinking one's engrained ideas and how that enhances progress both internally and with others. For me it emphasized how limited I'll be in shaping the world without remaining curious about it. </td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td> <br> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/sapiens.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/23692271-sapiens?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=hdkCdO7UAr&rank=1">Sapiens</a> <br> by Yuval Noah Harari </td>
		<td> Wide-ranging account covering the entire timeline of our species and the formation of human societies. The perspetive here felt encouraging, as I'm generally concerned about our civilization's trajectory entering the digital age. </td>
	</tr>
</table>

## Spirituality

<table>
	<tr>
		<td> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/power_of_now.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>
		<td> <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6708.The_Power_of_Now?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=NA7OIbrJSV&rank=1">The Power of Now</a> <br> by Eckhart Tolle </td>

	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td> <br> <img style="float: left;" src="/assets/img/2205_books/be_here_now.jpg" width="100" height="100"> </td>

		<td> Illustrative depictions of conscious experience to help shepherd a sense of the sacred into everyday existence. I really like how saliently Ram Dass reconciles the underlying spiritual connection between different religions. </td>
	</tr>
